# AI Superpowers by Kai-Fu Lee — BS-Scored Summary

**Author:** Kai-Fu Lee (2018)
**Topics:** US-China AI competition, job automation, implementation vs research
**Overall BS Score:** 5.2/10
**Verdict:** Partially right on China/US dynamics, too aggressive on job automation timelines

## Overview

AI Superpowers made two big predictions: China would challenge US AI dominance through implementation advantage, and AI would automate 40% of jobs within 15 years. Eight years later, we can check both.

## Key Claims

| Claim | BS Score | Rating | Verdict |
|-------|----------|--------|---------|
| China will overtake US in AI implementation | ~5.0 | Mixed | Partially validated — DeepSeek, Qwen competitive but China hasn't overtaken |
| 40% job automation in 15 years | ~5.0 | Mixed | 8 years in, actual displacement is single-digit %. Far behind pace |
| US leads research, China leads implementation | ~4.5 | Mixed | Was accurate in 2018; gap has narrowed with DeepSeek's research contributions |

## Deep Dive: The 40% Prediction

Lee's central economic prediction is the most testable claim in the book. He predicted 40% of jobs would be automated or significantly impacted by AI within 15 years (by ~2033). As of 2026:
- AI coding tools assist ~35% of PRs at some companies but haven't eliminated coding jobs
- Customer service automation has grown but not displaced 40% of workers
- New job categories (prompt engineering, AI oversight) have been created
- Overall labor market impact is estimated at 5-10%, not 40%

The prediction may eventually be partially right (AI is accelerating) but the timeline was too aggressive by at least 5-10 years.

## Who Should Read This

Useful for understanding US-China AI dynamics and the implementation vs research distinction. Read the geopolitical analysis; discount the automation timeline by 2-3x.
