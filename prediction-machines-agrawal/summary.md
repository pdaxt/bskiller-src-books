## Overview

Three University of Toronto economists argue that AI is fundamentally a prediction technology. When prediction gets cheap, it changes decision-making, business strategy, and job design. No hype, no doom — just microeconomics applied to AI. One of the few AI business books that aged well.

## Deep Dive: Prediction as the Core Framework

The book's central insight — AI is cheap prediction — is the cleanest lens for understanding LLMs:
- GPT-4 literally predicts the next token
- Image generators predict pixel distributions
- Recommendation engines predict preferences
- Self-driving cars predict what other road users will do

When prediction is cheap, you use it everywhere — even where you wouldn't have used it before. This explains why AI is being applied to tasks that don't look like "prediction" (writing, coding, design). The framework predicted the current AI application explosion better than most.

The economists' take on complements was also prescient: when prediction is cheap, **judgment** (deciding what to do with the prediction) becomes the scarce resource. This is exactly the human-in-the-loop reality of 2026.

The single best AI book for business leaders. No technical background needed. The economic framework is durable and practical. If you read one book about AI's business impact, make it this one.
