# The Big Nine by Amy Webb — BS-Scored Summary

**Author:** Amy Webb (2019)
**Topics:** AI geopolitics, tech giants, AI governance, future scenarios
**Overall BS Score:** 5.5/10
**Verdict:** Correctly identified the key AI players and geopolitical dynamics, but scenario planning was too neat and missed key developments

## Overview

Webb identifies nine companies controlling AI's future: six American (Google, Microsoft, Amazon, Facebook, IBM, Apple) and three Chinese (Baidu, Alibaba, Tencent). She presents three future scenarios (optimistic, pragmatic, catastrophic) and argues for better AI governance. Published before the LLM revolution reshuffled the deck.

## Deep Dive: Who Actually Controls AI in 2026

Webb's "Big Nine" in 2019 vs reality in 2026:
- **Google/DeepMind** — Yes, still dominant (Gemini)
- **Microsoft** — Yes, but via OpenAI investment, not internal research
- **Amazon** — Mostly through AWS infrastructure, not frontier models
- **Meta** — Yes, open-source play with Llama changed the game
- **IBM** — No. Watson was shuttered. IBM is irrelevant in frontier AI
- **Apple** — Late entrant. Apple Intelligence in 2024 was modest
- **Baidu** — Ernie Bot exists but isn't competitive globally
- **Alibaba** — Qwen models are competitive; better call than IBM
- **Tencent** — Minor player in frontier AI

**Missing from the Big Nine:** OpenAI (most important AI company), Anthropic (Claude), Nvidia (controls the hardware layer), DeepSeek (Chinese dark horse), Mistral (European contender). The field exploded beyond nine players.

## Who Should Read This

Useful for the geopolitical framing and governance arguments. The US-China analysis holds up. Skip the scenario chapters — reality chose "none of the above." Read alongside more recent analysis of the post-ChatGPT landscape.
